Boiled Veggies

Craving a healthy and hearty salad? Try this easy Boiled Green Vegetables Recipe! If you are a fan of vegetable salads, then you have to try this easy boiled green vegetables recipe. Prepared with cabbage, beans, cauliflower and salt, this plain boiled veggie salad gives you the nutrients without the extra fat. If you are a newbie in the kitchen, you can still nail this super easy boiled green vegetable recipe. This easy, simple vegetarian dish is high on fibre and a great food for summers. This easy and quick recipe can be best served during brunch, dinners and buffet. The best part of this recipe is that you can pair this recipe with steamed or boiled rice for a healthy meal and also serve as a side dish with heavy vegetarian or non vegetarian meals. You can add vegetables of your choice and even ingredients like paneer or tofu.



How to make Boiled Green Vegetables

Ingredients of Boiled Green Vegetables

4 Servings

4 cup cabbage

4 cup cauliflower

salt as required

4 cup bean

4 tablespoon virgin olive oil

8 tablespoon lemon juice

For Toppings

1 carrot

Step 1 Wash and prepare the veggies, Firstly, place cabbage, cauliflower and beans over a chopping board and chop their roots off. Now, wash them under running water and keep them aside.

Step 2 Chop and boil the veggies Next, place the vegetables again on the chopping board and slice them. Now, add sliced vegetables into the cooker and pour enough water to submerge the vegetables. Boil the sliced vegetables for 7-8 minutes or until tender.

Step 3 Cover with dressing & serve Once boiled, transfer the boiled vegetables into a large bowl and pour olive oil in it. Mix them well. Garnish the vegetables with the chopped pieces of carrot. Now drizzle lemon juice and sprinkle salt in the bowl. Serve!

Tips, You can also choose to just blanch the cabbage and green beans while boiling the cauliflower. This will ensure that there is a crunchy element in the salad. Feel free to add more veggies to the salad as per your taste. If you do not want any added fat in your salad, you can skip the olive oil. For the best flavour, you can use sea salt or Himalayan pink salt in the salad.
